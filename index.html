Research Overview

Project IRIS is an umbrella research initiative focused on the intersection of automated scientific discovery and biological systems simulation. The project utilizes a local computational stack to model complex biological success probabilities and mechanistic simulations.

Cloning Feasibility Model (CFM) v0.40

A functional stochastic risk assessment tool developed locally to quantify success rates in mammalian cloning through Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer (SCNT).

Methodology:
Monte Carlo Simulation (2,000 draws/run)
Outputs:
P05 / P50 / P95 Uncertainty Bands
  • Sensitivity Analysis: Automated calculation of gate correlation to identify biological bottlenecks (e.g., placenta vs. embryo cleavage).
  • Evidence Ingestion: Dynamic loading via local evidence.csv with machine-readable run logs for reproducibility.
  • Current Metric: 0.57% mean success probability, aligning with historical SCNT data.

Systems Architecture

  • Bio-Simulation Engine (BSE): A mechanistic PK/PD biological simulator presented at Lambton College's "Project: Pitch It!" competition.
  • Neural Self-Consistent System (NSCS): A recursive discovery framework designed for symbolic regression and scientific optimization.
