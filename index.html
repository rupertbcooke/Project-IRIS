Active Research Simulators

PRODUCTION: V0.40

Cloning Feasibility Model (CFM)

A probabilistic gate-based model of SCNT (Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer) designed to convert "cloning" from a qualitative concept into a formal mathematical structure.

  • Monte Carlo Methodology: Executes 2,000 stochastic draws per benchmark, pulling parameter values from evidence.csv to produce P05/P50/P95 outcome distributions.
  • 0.57% Mean Success: Current calibration point falls within historically observed ranges, validating the model against real biological constraints.
  • Bottleneck Detection: Utilizing a sensitivity layer to compute correlations between individual gates (e.g., placental development) and final success to guide engineering focus.
PROTOTYPE: PHYSIOLOGY

Bio-Simulation Engine (BSE)

A mechanistic "Virtual Physiology" engine representing organs as coupled dynamical systems rather than black-box models.

  • Multi-Organ Architecture: Explicit state variables (concentration, immune activity, toxicity thresholds) for Liver, Kidney, and Target tissues linked by metabolic exchange flows.
  • PK/PD Modeling: Coupled ODE state evolution modeling drug absorption, distribution, and functional response (ADME).
  • Cross-Species Scaling: Active research phase testing Rabbit → Human allometric parameter scaling to demonstrate generalizability across biological systems.

Discovery & Optimization

LOOP: RECURSIVE

Neural Self-Consistent System (NSCS)

A closed-loop equation discovery system designed to improve mechanistic simulators by enforcing self-consistency between structure and observed dynamics.

  • Symbolic Search: Utilizes regression to generate candidate governing equations (ODEs) that respect structural assumptions like conservation and monotonicity.
  • Neural Residual Correction: A neural network learns the delta between mechanistic predictions and observed trajectories, merging them into a final "Mechanistic + Residual" configuration.
  • Feedback Mechanism: Rejects unstable candidates and injects improved logic back into the BSE for iterative model refinement.
